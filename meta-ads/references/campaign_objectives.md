# campaign_objectives

> How to choose and use Meta (Facebook/Instagram) campaign objectives — the three objective categories, every specific objective, when to use each, and the build sequence that aligns optimization with your real goal. Sourced from *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*.

## Core frameworks

### Facebook Flight Plan — staged campaign blueprint

A progressive "take off then climb" blueprint that adds ad types and objectives as you accumulate conversions and data. Conversions can be leads, sales, or any defined conversion event. (Stages from *First Two Stages of the Facebook Flight Plan*; Stage 3 from Ch. 15.)

**Stage 1 — 0 to 100 conversions (takeoff).** Run only two proven campaigns to gain momentum, traffic, conversions, and — most of all — DATA. Keep it simple and novice-doable; don't build warm audiences yet.
1. **"Like" Ad Campaign** — page-like ad. CTA asks the user to click "Like" for cutting-edge tips/resources from someone "in the trenches." NO external destination; after the Like, the user keeps scrolling inside Facebook.
2. **Website Conversions Link Post Ad** — a clickable Link Post Ad driving traffic to a simple landing page to generate conversions. Normally run under the **Website Conversions** objective. Alternatives:
   - **Boosted Post** to a landing page — note the algorithm optimizes on *Engagement*, not Conversions, but you still get momentum/traffic/conversions/data.
   - **Lead Ad** — user fills the form right inside the newsfeed; Facebook pre-populates contact fields to reduce friction. Users are NOT auto-redirected to your confirmation page; they stay on Facebook with a button to optionally visit your site.
   - Can target warm and/or cold audiences depending on situation.

**Stage 2 — 101 to 1,000 conversions (climb).** Once you better understand how things work, layer on a third ad type. Guideline, not a hard rule — some may be ready after only 20–25 conversions. The third type is a **video ad**, but it does NOT have to be video:
   - **Long Copy Link Post Ad** (value-based / story-based, like a mini blog post in the post), OR
   - **Content with Call-to-Action (CTA) Video Ad.**
   - Which to use depends on your situation, assets, style, landing-page content, which ad type you started with, and how big your "ask" is.

**Stage 3 — (Ch. 15).** Introduces video ads, retargeting hot audiences, and **Dynamic Product Ads (DPAs)** at the end of Phase 3 (see Tactics).

### Three Categories of Facebook Campaign Objectives (Ch. 26)

Your objective is simply what you want people to do when they see your ad. Facebook groups all objectives into three buckets mapped to the customer journey; the algorithm puts your ad in front of people most likely to achieve the objective you set. Each category has specific ad formats and runs on specific platforms. First decide the category, then pick the specific objective.

1. **AWARENESS** — generate interest/awareness in your business, product, or service.
   - **Brand Awareness** — shows ads to people more likely to pay attention.
   - **Reach** — maximize number of people reached + control frequency.
2. **CONSIDERATION** — get people thinking more, seeking info, interacting more than awareness ads. Five objectives: **Traffic, App Installs, Engagement, Video Views, Lead Generation.** (Most marketers start here — may or may not be the best choice.)
3. **CONVERSION** — drive purchase/use/higher commitment. Three objectives: **Conversions, Product Catalog Sales, Store Visits.** Conversions are the "holy grail" — put a dollar in, get at least $1.01 back out.

### Four Most-Used Ad Types for Conversions — by commitment level (Ch. 17)

Match the right offer to the right ad type. The higher the ask, the more pre-framing and longer copy/video needed. Listed in reverse order of commitment:
1. Short copy ad to a blog post.
2. Short copy ad to a simple lead magnet.
3. Long copy ad to a complex lead magnet, webinar, or product.
4. Video ad to a lead magnet, webinar, or product.

### Facebook Ad Creation Order: Campaign > Ad Set > Ad (Ch. 11)

The standard three-level build sequence:
1. **CAMPAIGN** — set the objective (e.g., Page Likes); name it descriptively (e.g., "Like Campaign").
2. **AD SET** — define audience, placements, and budget. (How you name ad sets matters more than the campaign name.)
3. **AD** — the creative: pick images and write copy.

## Tactics & best practices

**Pixel-driven optimization (The Power of the Pixel)**
- Selecting an objective + a specific conversion event tells Facebook's AI to find more users like your converters. Example: Website Conversions optimized for webinar registrations → Facebook shows ads to people like those registering.
- The pixel feeds data back on every fulfilled objective; targeting improves after every single conversion.

**Awareness objectives**
- **Reach for local/physical businesses** — maximize reach over the campaign, cap frequency, and use location targeting. Use **"Everyone in this location"** to target only people traveling nearby. (Ch. 26)
- Both Awareness objectives can include CTA buttons (but ads won't be optimized for clicks). Formats: single image, single video, carousel, slideshow. Brand Awareness adds Canvas.

**Consideration objectives**
- **Traffic** — best for content amplification (a great blog post) or driving app engagement. Optimizes for cheap clicks, not conversions. (Ch. 26)
- **App Installs** — nearly identical to Traffic but sends clicks to an app store; optimize for mobile traffic. (Ch. 26)
- **Engagement** — three sub-types: Boost your posts (post engagement), Promote your page (page likes), Raise event attendance (event responses). (Ch. 26)
- **Page Post Engagement to pre-test content** — Facebook shows it to people likely to engage; use it to see if content resonates, then later turn the winning post into a conversion ad. (Ch. 26)
- **Run multiple audiences against one post** — boosting from the page timeline gives only ONE audience. Use the **Page Post Engagement** objective in Ads Manager and duplicate the ad set to run many audiences simultaneously (e.g., $1/day vs. social media marketers, $1/day vs. fans of Mari Smith, plus the boosted post's audience). (Dennis Yu / More Boosted Posts)
- **Event Responses** — targets people likely to join a Facebook-hosted event; auto-adds it to their calendar. Great for free public events, grand openings, festivals. RULE: for **paid events use Conversions instead.** (Ch. 26)
- **Lead Generation (lead form ads)** — captures contact info via an in-Facebook form that auto-populates; eliminates the landing page and the click-to-opt-in drop-off ("No bounce rate here"). Though technically not categorized under Conversions, treat it as one. (Ch. 26)

**Conversion objectives**
- **Conversions** — requires the Facebook pixel installed; tells Facebook to show your ad to people highly likely to convert. Reports cost per conversion (e.g., cost per lead). (Ch. 26)
- **Product Catalog Sales + Dynamic Product Ads (DPAs)** — best for ecommerce / multi-item sellers (e.g., travel). Build one ad template that auto-pulls images/details from your catalog and shows the most relevant products based on browsing behavior, without configuring each ad. Runs on all Facebook platforms. (Ch. 26)
- **DPAs for retargeting (Ch. 15)** — auto-promote the specific products a visitor viewed (e.g., set rules so anyone who hit add-to-cart/checkout for "Fall Selection Boots" gets reminded before stock runs out). "Low-hanging fruit" — get something running, get fancy later.
- **Store Visits for multi-location businesses** — combines physical locations + geo-targeting + people with location services on. Setup: (1) add all business locations in Facebook (bulk upload available); (2) target a specific mile radius OR let it update dynamically by desired reach; (3) overlay custom/interest/demographic audiences per location. Ads dynamically update per location. (Ch. 26)

**Video: Conversion vs. Video Views/Engagement (Ch. 26)**
- If a video's SOLE purpose is conversion, usually start with the **Conversion** objective.
- But a super-engaging/viral video that also converts can deliver better ROI/CPA on **Video Views**, **Page Post Engagement**, or just by boosting — because Facebook serves a larger, broader audience at much lower CPM/CPC. No hard rule: test. Such a video should have a very high Like-to-Share ratio (~1:1 or better, e.g., 1,248 reactions / 1,276 shares).

**Local business strategy (Ch. 12)**
- **Branding campaign** — allocate a small portion of budget; may have no direct CTA. Builds top-of-mind awareness and "invisible targeting lists." Examples: boost a restaurant post ("Come in, mention Facebook, get a free appetizer"); real estate/auto — boost a video or article so it runs all month.
- **Lead Ad funnel** — opt-in without leaving the newsfeed (no website needed). Only ~20–25% click through to your thank-you page.

**Page Like campaigns — allocate 5–10% of budget (Ch. 26).** Four reasons:
1. **Twice the newsfeed frequency** — up to 4 ads/day to fans vs. 2/day to non-fans.
2. **Social proof** — "so-and-so and 20 others like this page" creates FOMO.
3. **Connection targeting** — run ads to friends of fans; "Advanced Combinations" lets you target an interest (e.g., "Tony Robbins") AND friends-of-people-connected to your page.
4. **Highest ROI** — fans are a warm market (as long as you didn't buy fake likes).

**Choosing the right objective (Ch. 26)**
1. Think through what EXACTLY you want to accomplish.
2. Choose the objective that fits best (may depend on the quality of your specific ad).
3. If two+ could work and you have budget, TEST them.
4. No budget/time? Go with the objective you have the best feeling about.
- KEY PRINCIPLE: A great ad + great offer will likely succeed regardless of objective; a sub-par ad/offer will likely fail regardless of objective.
- Don't reflexively default to Conversion for every campaign — each objective does something different.

## Checklists

**Before building any campaign**
- [ ] Decide which of the three categories (Awareness / Consideration / Conversion) the ad serves.
- [ ] Drill down to the specific objective within that category.
- [ ] Confirm the objective matches your ACTUAL goal (don't pick Traffic/Engagement if you want sales).
- [ ] Choose the objective BEFORE building — you cannot change it on a live campaign.
- [ ] If running a Conversions objective: confirm the Facebook pixel is installed.
- [ ] If running a paid event: use Conversions, not Event Responses.
- [ ] If two+ objectives could work and budget allows: plan a test.
- [ ] After placing the order, remember the new campaign is paused — activate it (and add more ad sets first if desired).

**Store Visits setup**
- [ ] Add all business locations in Facebook (bulk upload if many).
- [ ] Set a fixed mile radius OR a dynamic radius based on desired reach.
- [ ] Overlay custom/interest/demographic audiences per location.

## Pitfalls to avoid

- **You cannot change the objective on a live campaign (Ch. 26).** Pick the wrong one and you must rebuild from scratch. Decide BEFORE building.
- **Wrong objective = wrong result (Troubleshooting).** Running Traffic or Page Post Engagement when you want sales gets you traffic/engagement, not conversions. When you see high social shares but low conversions, first check that the objective matches the goal — switch to a conversion objective.
- **Traffic optimizes for clicks, not leads/sales (Ch. 26).** The "if I just get enough traffic I'll convert something" mindset is flawed — you get tons of cheap clicks and no conversions; the ad did exactly what you asked. Don't measure Traffic by conversions.
- **Don't default to the Conversion objective for every campaign (Ch. 26).** Direct-response reflex; other objectives often fit better within an overall strategy.
- **Boosted posts won't optimize for Conversions (Flight Plan).** The algorithm optimizes them for Engagement — fine for early momentum/data, but not for true conversion optimization.
- **Lead Ads don't auto-redirect to your thank-you/confirmation page (Ch. 26 / Flight Plan).** Users stay on Facebook; only ~20–25% click through.
- **New campaigns are paused on creation (Ch. 28).** They won't spend until you activate them.
- **Use Conversions (not Event Responses) for paid events (Ch. 26).**
