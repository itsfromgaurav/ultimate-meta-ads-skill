# account_setup

> How to stand up the foundational Meta (Facebook/Instagram) advertising assets — Page, Business Manager, ad account, team/partner access, and pixel — before launching any campaigns. Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*.

## Core frameworks

### Foundational setup sequence before launching ads
The complete, ordered foundation that must be in place before any campaign goes live. The author built a new health/fitness brand with three divisions — (1) a local personal-training gym, (2) a digital product business (meal plans & workout programs), and (3) an ecommerce division (physical products) — from zero and grew it on essentially ONE Facebook ad (still running, the only ad running) by following this exact sequence (Ch. 2 / Ch. 3):

1. **Buy a new domain** (e.g., at GoDaddy).
2. **Install WordPress** / set up the site.
3. **Build the landing pages.**
4. **Create a new Facebook Page** (business Page, not a personal profile).
5. **Create a new Business Manager** account.
6. **Create a new ad account.**
7. **Set up tracking** (install/configure the pixel and conversion tracking) — do this ASAP after the ad account exists (Ch. 25).
8. **Create a lead magnet** for each business division.
9. **Create and publish the campaign(s).**

Minimum prerequisites that must exist before running campaigns (Ch. 9): your Facebook **Page**, your **ad account**, and your **unique selling system**.

### Create a Facebook Business Page (step-by-step)
(Ch. 9)

1. Go to `https://www.facebook.com/pages/create/` and choose a Page type.
2. When prompted, choose a **Category** (you can change the type and category later, so don't worry if you pick wrong).
3. Give your Page a **name** and click **Get Started**.
4. Before sharing/promoting, add a **profile picture**, a **cover photo**, and a **description** of what your business is about.
5. Create a **username** for your Page so people can find it in search (it can't already be in use, so you may have to try a few before getting a free one).
6. **Post some content** to show you're active and ready for business.

### Set up Facebook Business Manager (step-by-step)
Business Manager is Facebook's recommended platform for managing your ads. It lets you easily grant access to anyone who needs to work on your Page or ads, and by having all your Facebook assets linked in it, it signals to Facebook that you're a legitimate business (Ch. 9).

1. Go to `https://business.facebook.com/` and click **Create Account**.
2. Log in with your personal Facebook details.
3. Add your **business name**, **your name**, and **business email** to create your Business Manager account.
4. Click **Business Settings** to add your Page and ad account.

### Add your Page to Business Manager
(Ch. 9) In **Business Settings**, hover over the column of icons on the left, select **Pages** first, then click **Add New Pages** → **Add a Page**. Start typing the name of your Facebook Page and it should auto-populate. Click **Add Page**. Because you're already an admin on your Page, it is approved automatically.

### Add an existing ad account to Business Manager
(Ch. 9) Hover over the left-hand icons, click **Ad Accounts** → **Add New Ad Accounts** → **Add an Ad Account**. You'll need the **Ad Account ID**: open the ad account in a different tab and look at the URL — you'll see `ACT=` followed by a string of numbers; those numbers are the Ad Account ID. Copy/paste that string into the account ID field in Business Manager and click **Add Ad Account**.

### Create a NEW ad account in Business Manager
(Ch. 9) Hover over the left-hand icons, click **Ad Accounts** → **Add New Ad Accounts** → **Create a New Ad Account**. You'll be prompted for a **name**, **time zone**, and **currency**, then click **Create Ad Account**.
> **Pitfall:** You CANNOT change the **time zone** or **currency** once the account is created — get them right the first time.

### Add People to Business Manager (team members)
(Ch. 9) Hover over the left-hand icons and choose the top one, **People** → **Add New People**. Enter the person's **email address**, then select whether they will be an **admin** or an **employee**. Click **Add People**. Assign the relevant **Pages** and **Ad Accounts** to them and click Next through the screens. They receive an email invitation; once they accept, they can work on the assets you allocated.

### Add an agency/consultant as a Partner (via Business ID)
(Ch. 9) To grant an outside agency or consultant access to your Page/Ad Account, use their **Business ID**. Find a Business ID in the Business Manager URL — it's the string of numbers after `?business_id=`. To add: select your Page or Ad Account in Business Manager settings → click **Assign Partner** → choose **"Connect your Page/Ad Account using your partner's business ID instead"** → insert the ID they gave you. They'll be added with whatever **Role** you choose.

## Tactics & best practices

- **Complete the Page profile before promoting it** — add a profile picture, cover photo, description, username, and some posted content before driving any traffic, so the Page shows you're active and ready for business (Ch. 9).
- **Set up your pixel ASAP after creating the ad account.** A new ad account prompts you to set up the pixel; just follow the prompts. Doing it early means you start accumulating tracking, audience, and optimization data as soon as possible (Ch. 25).
- **Link all assets in Business Manager** — it makes granting team/agency access easy and signals to Facebook that you're a legitimate business (Ch. 9).

## Pitfalls to avoid

- **Time zone & currency are permanent.** When creating a new ad account, you cannot change the time zone or currency afterward — set them correctly the first time (Ch. 9).
- **Don't advertise from a personal Profile.** You must run ads from a **Page**, not a personal **Profile**. Quick way to tell them apart: **Profiles have "Friends," Pages have "Likes."** (Ch. 9)
- **Don't promote an empty Page.** Build it out fully (images, description, username, content) before driving traffic to it (Ch. 9).
- **Don't risk your ad account.** Treat the Facebook ad account as a valuable business asset: "Make sure you thoroughly read Facebook ad policies and abide by them at all times so that you can guarantee the health and longevity of your ad account and ultimately of your business." (Ch. 17)
