# scaling
> How to turn small, proven Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ad campaigns into large ones while holding target CPA — using a structured five-method system instead of brute-force budget hikes.

## Core frameworks

### Facebook Flight Plan (foundation for scale)
Scaling assumes a sound foundation. Executing the first two Flight Plan stages correctly (the process in Chapters 16-20) builds a foundation for **massive scale and long-term ROI** for both campaigns and the business (Ch. 16-20).

- The next stage builds a true **perpetual customer-generation system** spanning the entire customer journey: awareness, engagement, fans, leads, sales, recovered (scooped-up) abandoned sales, repeat sales, and brand goodwill.
- Principle: get the foundation right *first*, then scale on top of it.

### Five-Tier Scaling System (Ralph Burns)
An ordered system of five scaling methods to grow small successful campaigns into large ones while maintaining target CPA. There is no one-size-fits-all to scaling; together these methods reach large audiences without Facebook's "jerky rollercoaster" behavior. They work best alongside the proprietary **Michigan Method** campaign-building process. **Try each method in order — 1 before 2, 2 before 3, etc.** Combining all five at once is called **Massive Rapid Scaling**.

The five tiers, in order:
1. Duplication Scaling
2. Audience Scaling
3. Alteration Scaling
4. Large Budget Scaling
5. Small Budget Scaling

---

#### Tier 1 — Duplication Scaling
Make exact copies of a well-performing ad set, keeping the **same starting budget** and the **same Post ID**.

**When to use:** CPA is **$100 or lower**. "Performing Well" = at least **10 conversions** in an ad set below desired CPA — have enough data and feel confident before scaling. Works best on **large audiences, ideally >1 million**; smaller audiences support fewer ad sets and frequency rises faster (raising costs).

**Process:**
1. In Ads Manager, set calendar to **last seven days**.
2. Sort ad sets by CPA, smallest to largest.
3. Screen by volume of conversions; scroll to ad sets with significant conversion counts and select all ads at or below your CPA.
4. Highlight those ads, click **Create Similar Ad Set**.
5. When the new window opens, review and press **Place Order**.
6. Facebook auto-appends "-copy" to ad set names — search that phrase and rename all with **"DUP" plus the date or a version number** to track duplicates.
7. Hit **Edit**, scroll to **Budget and Schedule**, enter the daily budget.
8. Select all duplicates, click **Enter Post ID**, insert the original ad's Post ID, press **Submit**.

**Key rule:** Always duplicate the **ORIGINAL** ad, not the duplicate ad sets.

---

#### Tier 2 — Audience Scaling
Broaden reach into new audiences.

**Advanced execution:** Duplicate your entire **Michigan Method** campaign, then replace the original **20 interests with 20 new interests**, keeping the same creative.

---

#### Tier 3 — Alteration Scaling
Like Duplication Scaling, but make **minor, data-driven** creative/copy tweaks to give the ad a fresh look for the same audience without changing it significantly.

**Tweak examples:** change a statement into a question; insert brackets or emojis; combine the image from one winning ad with the copy of another winning ad; swap the image for a similar-themed one. Base every change on data — use your best-performing content (including hook and offer) presented slightly differently.

**Process:**
1. Set calendar to **last seven days**.
2. Sort ad sets by CPA smallest to largest.
3. Screen by conversion volume for ad sets with significant conversions and lowest CPA.
4. Highlight those ads, click **Create Similar Ad Set**.
5. Enter new images or new copy.
6. Rename the ad to reflect the variation (e.g., `[name] Copy 7 Img 3`).
7. Hit **Review Changes** and place order.

**Purpose / rules:** Get more ads in front of the same audience with slight variations. Keep audiences **large** so different ads aren't seen too frequently; varied content keeps your ad out of viewers' "seen that" blind spot.

**Advanced execution:** After duplicating your campaign, create **3 new creatives** and **3 new pieces of ad copy** — combining them yields **9 different ad variations** (each copy with each creative). Use the original 20 interests in the original Michigan Method campaign.

---

#### Tier 4 — Large Budget Scaling
Duplicate winning ad sets into **NEW campaigns** and increase budget **5-20x** the original.

**Best practice:** Set daily budget to **2-3x your target CPA**.

**Process:**
1. Duplicate your lowest-CPA ad sets and insert them into a new campaign.
2. Increase daily budget to your comfort level, ideally **2-3x CPA**.

**Cautions & nuances:**
- Be extremely cautious — you spend much more and ramping budgets can wreak havoc with the algorithm.
- Being more aggressive than 3x CPA works about **1 in 5 times** — only worth trying with a really hot offer.
- Disclosure: one advertiser always starts at **$300/day** and usually gets a **$3 cost per lead** — but they have amazing offers; guidelines are experience-based, not absolute.
- **Timing:** Start new campaigns in the **mornings** (little difference between midnight and early morning). Avoid **evenings** — Facebook spends only a small portion of the daily budget before day's end, skewing results.

**Ninja trick:** When increasing daily budgets by large amounts (e.g., $100 → $300), change the **lower end of your age range by one year** — Facebook treats this as a different audience and won't see your ads as competing with your others.

---

#### Tier 5 — Small Budget Scaling
The most conservative method. Instead of jumping to 2-3x CPA, raise the budget **~20% every three days** until CPA exceeds target, then roll back.

**Process:**
1. Set calendar to **last seven days**.
2. Sort ad sets by CPA smallest to largest.
3. Screen by conversion volume for ad sets with significant conversions and lowest CPA.
4. Click **Edit** and increase daily budget by **15-20%**; Save and close.
5. Check **every three days** that CPA is below target; if so, increase daily budget another **15-20%**.
6. When target CPA is exceeded, **roll back by at least two increments** by entering a lower budget.

**Notes:**
- Solves Facebook's dislike of sudden large budget jumps.
- Results may be skewed for **72 hours** while Facebook adjusts; three days is a guide — increase increment speed if the campaign settles faster.
- Very labor intensive — managing **more than ten ad sets** this way becomes unmanageable; useful on a tight budget.
- View increase history in Ads Manager under the individual ad set by clicking **amount spent**; hover the green line to see the budget before you exceeded target CPA.

**Example progression:** $10/day → $12/day → wait 3 days, review → add another 20% if doing well → 3 days later you could double it. Keep rising ~20% every three days; if you exceed target CPA, roll back two increments.

---

#### Massive Rapid Scaling (combine all tiers)
To go really big really fast, combine all five strategies **simultaneously**. Example: use **duplication** to add ad sets within the same campaign *and* create a brand-new campaign; use **audience scaling** to broaden into new audiences; create more ads/ad sets/campaigns via **alteration scaling**; and increase spend via **large budget scaling** — all at once.

## Tactics & best practices

- **Build a cloneable system, not personal talent ("play the orchestra").** Long-term, scalable success comes from a repeatable, systematized methodology others can execute — not lone-star brilliance. (Ch. 4) The book's twins parable: James built a teaching system, trained 12 junior instructors, and scaled beyond his own hours (packed calendar, mile-long waiting list, 10,000 sq ft studio); Ricky relied solely on personal talent and stayed stuck. Mirrors Steve Jobs ("I play the orchestra") and companies at the Microsoft/Amazon level spending **upwards of $12 billion/year on R&D** because systems reliably produce marketplace-winning results. Applied to Facebook ads: clone a repeatable winning approach.
- **Scale large audiences for headroom.** Duplication and alteration both perform best on audiences **>1 million** — smaller audiences cap the number of ad sets and push frequency (and cost) up faster.
- **Always work from data.** Sort by CPA (smallest→largest), screen by conversion volume, and scale only the lowest-CPA, highest-confidence ad sets.
- **Time new campaigns for mornings**, not evenings, so budget pacing doesn't skew early results.

## Pitfalls to avoid

- **Naive budget multiplication.** Going $10/day → $100/day does NOT yield a proportional (10x) revenue increase at the same CPA. Scaling by budget alone feels like a "jerky rollercoaster": results go up, down, get stuck, sometimes recover, sometimes don't; Facebook may stop showing your ads; conversion costs can skyrocket. The exact cause is unknown (likely an algorithm quirk) — like the Soup Nazi, once your "order" (budget) is set, sudden changes can make Facebook "punish" the campaign. Use the structured five-method system instead.
- **Skipping the order of tiers.** Try method 1 before 2, 2 before 3, etc. — don't jump straight to aggressive budget scaling.
- **Over-aggressive Large Budget Scaling.** Exceeding 3x CPA only works ~1 in 5 times; ramping budgets too fast can wreak havoc with the algorithm.
- **Launching campaigns in the evening.** Facebook spends only part of the daily budget before day's end, skewing your read on performance.
- **Scaling on too-small audiences.** Caps the number of ad sets you can run and pushes frequency/cost up quickly.
- **Running Small Budget Scaling at scale.** It's very labor intensive; more than ~10 ad sets becomes unmanageable.
- **Reading results inside the 72-hour adjustment window.** Allow Facebook ~3 days to settle before judging a budget change.
- **Relying on personal talent instead of a cloneable system** — it caps long-term scale (Ch. 4).
