# boosted_posts

> Facebook's "Easy Button" for getting started with ads: how to boost a post, why boosting is the fastest cheap way to test hooks and audiences, and Dennis Yu's advanced amplification system (switch-boost, unicorn babies, greatest-hits re-boosting) for turning proven "unicorn" posts into compounding reach.

## Core frameworks

### How to Boost a Post (Facebook's "Big Easy Button")
The simplest way to start advertising on Facebook — done directly from your business page. It is the lowest-barrier first step into Facebook advertising. (Facebook Easy Button: Boosted Posts)

1. **Add content** to your page.
2. **Click the big blue "Boost Post" button.**
3. **Select your audience.**
4. **Assign a budget.**

That's it. Note: the mechanics are easy, but boosting still requires the right content in front of the right people — the button lowers the barrier to testing, it does not replace fundamentals.

### Switch-Boost + Media Inception + Unicorn Baby (Dennis Yu amplification system)
Three components that fit together to take one winning post and compound its reach while preserving accumulated social proof. (More Boosted Posts — Dennis Yu)

1. **Switch-boost** — Originally boost a post to a broad interest/behavioral target to get high engagement cheaply (stimulating the algorithm) and build social proof. Then *switch* the audience of that same boosted post to a new, more strategic audience (e.g., an influencer/"media inception" audience). The post **keeps all existing social proof** (likes, comments, shares), creating a rolling snowball effect you can redirect at will. You can switch-boost multiple times — including to capitalize on current events (e.g., switch older boosted posts to Social Media Examiner fans for a couple of weeks before speaking at Social Media Marketing World).
2. **Media inception audience** — A strategic influencer/multiplier audience (people who can influence your customers) you redirect the post toward.
3. **Unicorn baby** — Once you have one unicorn (a home-run post), create many derivative variants by: (a) switching the audience of the boost, (b) duplicating the ad set to run against two audiences at once, or (c) creating derivative (similar/related) content to replicate the effect. When you earn media coverage, share/boost their post to your media inception targets to amplify and spawn more unicorn babies.

## Tactics & best practices

**Test fast and cheap**
- Boosting is the easiest way to quickly test different hooks — instead of guessing how your market will respond, let the Facebook data tell you what's good and bad.
- Boosted posts are great for: testing fast, testing with small budgets, testing different hooks, testing different audiences, building social proof, and engaging more with your market and the people who influence it.

**Only boost proven winners (quality over quantity)**
- Boosting is just **multiplying** a post: boost a video with bad organic results and you push terrible results to more people; boost one with great results and your numbers go through the roof.
- Measure organically **first**, then put ad dollars only behind proven winners.
- Larry Kim and Dennis Yu spend the bulk of their time cultivating winners ("unicorns") rather than coaxing "donkeys" to fly. Tune different audience combos for proven posts instead of creating tons of new posts.
- A home-run post is a **"unicorn."** You won't hit it out of the park every time — test many posts cheaply, find the rare winners, and amplify those.

**Re-boost evergreen "greatest hits"**
- Boosting isn't a one-time action. Keep a "greatest hits" list of your most successful posts — like favorite songs, replay them.
- If something worked well before, it'll work again as long as the content is evergreen. Look at what's done well over the last **24 months**, switch-boost it, and create similar versions. Far less effort than attempting a "moonshot" every day.

**Build social proof and authority**
- Boosting builds likes, shares, and fans — social proof that gives you authority because people trust you more when they see others trust you.
- Boosting posts and Like Campaigns are typically among the cheapest ways to build this social proof.

**Influence the influencers (journalist approach)**
- Approach boosted posts like a journalist: target the people who can influence your customers — magazine/radio/newspaper/TV staff, bloggers, conference attendees.
- Targeting these multipliers amplifies reach beyond direct customers.

**Local business top-of-mind awareness**
- Great for restaurants, dentists, chiropractors, lawyers, cosmetic surgeons, locksmiths, etc.
- Boost personal-story posts; it may not make the phone ring instantly, but the consistency and top-of-mind awareness mean people think of you when the need arises.

**No technical skill required**
- The pizzeria-owner case study: a non-technical owner (doesn't even know WordPress) succeeds simply by boosting photos of food to relevant local audiences — including targeting the audience of local-newspaper food critics. Boosting needs no technical skill and can target influencer audiences directly.

## Copy/templates

Local-business / top-of-mind boosted post (personal-story hook):
```

I've got three vans.
[Then tell the rest of your story, or talk about how you recently
solved a problem your potential customers might be experiencing.]
```


## Checklists

Dollar-a-day test-and-scale workflow:
- [ ] Publish posts organically and measure organic results first
- [ ] Stack worthy posts against each other by boosting each at $1/day for 7 days (or test each at $50 — Larry Kim's method)
- [ ] Compare reach and engagement across the boosted posts
- [ ] Identify the winners ("unicorns"); ignore the "donkeys"
- [ ] Put more spend behind the winners
- [ ] Switch-boost winners to strategic / influencer (media inception) audiences, preserving social proof
- [ ] Create "unicorn babies": switch audience, duplicate the ad set against a second audience, and/or make derivative content
- [ ] Add winners to a "greatest hits" list and re-boost evergreen ones (look back over the last 24 months)

## Pitfalls to avoid

- **Don't boost losers.** Boosting multiplies results — pushing a poor-performing post to more people just buys worse results at scale. Validate organically first.
- **Don't chase a daily "moonshot."** Coaxing "donkeys" to fly wastes effort; cultivating proven unicorns is higher ROI. Favor quality over quantity.
- **Don't treat the Boost button as a substitute for fundamentals.** It only lowers the barrier to testing — you still need the right content in front of the right people.
- **Don't treat boosting as one-and-done.** Re-boost and switch-boost evergreen winners; don't let proven content go stale.
