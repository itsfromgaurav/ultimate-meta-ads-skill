# adset_structure_budget

> How to structure, target, place, budget, and bid Meta ad sets — the level where most testing and ongoing optimization happens. Distilled from *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*.

## Core frameworks

### A.P.B. — Audience, Placement, Budget
The three core decisions every ad set requires, regardless of campaign objective (a play on law enforcement's "all-points bulletin") (A.P.B. chapter).

- **A**udience, **P**lacement, **B**udget are always the next three things you decide at the ad set level — you're describing the "suspect"/prospect you want to engage.
- Some objectives add extra components on top of A.P.B. (see the objective-specific settings under Tactics).
- The ad set level is where most ongoing changes/modifications are made and where the majority of variable testing happens.
- Large accounts can have hundreds or thousands of ad sets per campaign because of the flexibility available at this level.

### The Four Ad Set Groups (Targeting Game Plan)
Structure campaigns into four ad set categories covering warm and cold audiences so campaigns fire "on all four cylinders." Ideally target all four (Ch. 24).

1. **Your Fans (warm)** — strong ROI source and a generator of positive comments/shares/likes (social proof) on your ads — *only if fans were generated the right way*. Listed #1 because campaigns are often launched to fans first to get some "social love."
2. **Your Custom Audiences (warm)** — generally better ROI than cold audiences since these people already know your brand; power increases as size grows.
3. **1% Lookalike Audiences (cold)** — a great source for new prospects; more powerful as the source audience grows.
4. **Interests and Behaviors (cold)** — where most campaigns start; tell Facebook which audiences sound most like your prospects. Recommended starting point: 5–20 interests. Even successful accounts the agency takes over still use interest targeting. If just starting out, you may rely on #4 alone until you build the numbers to make the other three work.

**Sequenced rollout** (Ch. 24):
1. Start with Ad Set #4 — Interest Targeting.
2. As you build your audiences, add #1 (Fans) and #2 (Custom Audiences).
3. As you see conversions from #1 and #2, add #3 (1% Lookalikes).
This is the general best starting reference; customize to your business. Don't worry if you don't have all these assets yet — this is what to aim for.

### The Michigan Method
A proprietary campaign structure using many small-budget ad sets with exactly one ad each (Five-Tier Scaling System chapter).

- Uses **a lot of ad sets, all with small budgets — sometimes as many as 180**.
- **Only ONE ad per ad set.**
- For optimal performance, **target larger audiences (400,000–2 million)** so ad sets don't cannibalize one another or saturate the market.
- Beauty of it: within a short period you see which ad sets are failing and which deliver an awesome CPA. Kill the failures and build on the winners — "Killing the Ponies, and Feeding the Stallions." The five-tier scaling system is about "bulking-up your stallions."
- Advanced application: duplicate the entire campaign (which uses 20 interests) and swap the original 20 interests for 20 new ones, keeping the same creative.

## Tactics & best practices

**Audience & interest structure**
- Keep **one (or two related) interest per ad set** so you can measure how each interest performs — split interests into separate ad sets rather than combining them. Target ~1.5M reach per ad set for clean, comparable data (Ch. 11).
- Each ad set should still contain multiple ads for a good spread and valuable data — *except* the Michigan Method (one ad per ad set).
- **Smaller audiences (under ~400K, or under the 1M+ ideal):** keep multiple ad variations in rotation inside one ad set but only one ad live at a time. This is the *only* time more than one ad per ad set is recommended — it lets you swap ads when frequency gets too high and CPA starts increasing. Keep budgets small, and you may need to switch bidding from conversions to impressions (Ch. 23 / Five-Tier Scaling System).

**Placement**
- Ads can serve across **Facebook, Instagram, the Audience Network, and Sponsored Messages** via two modes: Automatic placements (Facebook chooses) or Edit placements (you choose).
- **Automatic placements** = Facebook's recommended default; gives the algorithm full control. A smart strategy for *new campaigns to cold traffic* — it reveals where your audience responds most, which you can apply to future manual placements.
- **Edit placements** lets you control device and platform. Choices cascade: device type (all / mobile only / desktop only) and campaign objective both constrain available platforms (e.g., desktop-only removes Instagram, which is mobile-only). FB placements: feeds, instant articles, in-stream videos, right column, suggested videos. IG placements: feed or Stories.
- **Advanced placement controls:** (1) specific mobile devices/OS (all mobile, Android only, iOS only, and/or WiFi-only); (2) exclude content categories that conflict with your brand on in-stream video, audience network, and instant articles; (3) block lists for specific websites/apps/publishers — once uploaded, reusable across multiple campaigns.
- **Like campaigns:** Instagram, Audience Network, and Messenger are ineligible — just use Automatic Placements. Mobile-only / non-mobile / device restrictions are allowed but rarely needed (Ch. 11).

**Budget & scheduling**
- Budget is set at the ad set level: **Daily** (recommended for ongoing control) or **Lifetime** (recommended for fixed-duration campaigns with a hard cap). You cannot switch types after choosing — you must duplicate the ad set to change.
- Lifetime budget unlocks **ad scheduling (dayparting)**. Running ads *all the time* usually performs best (the algorithm serves during the best windows), but scheduling lets you control exact times when needed.
- Use **scheduled start/end dates** to time launches/stops. Tip: don't launch late in a day — use a scheduled start to go live early the next morning.
- Estimated daily reach shown in Ads Manager/Power Editor scales with your budget.

**Bidding**
- Bid amount is set in optimization & delivery. **Automatic bid** (let Facebook get the most results) is the usual choice. **Manual bid** sets your target cost per result, with AVERAGE or MAXIMUM options — use Manual MAXIMUM when you have a hard cap on cost per result.
- Bid types: **CPC** (charged only on click; you set the max, starting at 1¢), **CPM** (pay per 1,000 impressions whether clicked or not — mainly brand advertising, not best for most advertisers), and **oCPM** (shows your ad to people most likely to take your desired action, auto-adjusting bid within budget). **oCPM is the default for all objectives except Brand Awareness and Reach.**
- Your ad competes in an auction against everyone targeting the same audience; bid is only one factor. After a few thousand impressions, CTR, Relevance Score, and likes/complaints affect cost. Facebook often charges *less* than you bid. A credit card is required before ads display.

**Objective-specific settings (beyond A.P.B.)**
- Brand awareness, post engagement, event response, video views, store visits → A.P.B. only.
- Reach, page likes, lead generation → choose the **Page**.
- App installs → choose your **app**. Product catalog → choose your **products**.
- Traffic → choose destination (website / Messenger / app); optional **offer** (pick the Page, then an existing or new offer).
- Conversion → choose the **conversion event** to optimize for.

**Like campaign specifics (Ch. 11)**
- Start at **$10/day per ad set** (a little less is fine), keep bidding automatic.
- Name each ad set by its targeting + placement so it's easy to identify; if you change targeting/placement before going live, update the name.
- Like campaigns should consume only **5–10% of total budget** (up to 20–25% in exceptional high-conversion cases). Set it and let it run so your audience always grows with fresh leads.

**Carousel ordering (Ch. 21)**
- **Uncheck** "show best performing image first" for sequential/story carousels meant to be viewed in order (e.g., the DM HQ steps carousel).
- **Check** it for feature/benefit carousels with no required order (e.g., TruConversion's software-benefit screens).

## Checklists

**Power User Tip — duplicate ad sets to target many interests fast (Ch. 28):**
- [ ] Click the paused campaign to open the Ad Sets tab.
- [ ] Select the first ad set you built and click **Duplicate**.
- [ ] In the duplicate window, leave the default **Keep Same Campaign** selected.
- [ ] Set **number of duplicates** to one less than your total interests (10 interests → 9 duplicates → 10 ad sets total).
- [ ] For status, choose **Keep Campaign Status Same as Duplicated**.
- [ ] Find the new ad sets ending in the word **Copy**.
- [ ] Select each copy, hit **Edit**, and add a new/different audience/interest to it.

**Power Editor — duplicate a campaign for new targeting groups (Ch. 11):**
- [ ] Find the campaign in Power Editor and select **Duplicate** at the top.
- [ ] Keep the same campaign — keep the status option checked.
- [ ] Hit **Create**; the new campaign appears with "Copy" in the title (don't retitle yet).
- [ ] Change **only the targeting** in this ad set (add custom audiences once you have them; for a new brand, add more interests and watch reach change).
- [ ] Repeat to create more ad sets for different interests; each keeps multiple ads for spread/data.
- [ ] Press **Review Changes**, hit **Continue**, then confirm all ad sets appear in Ads Manager.

**One-Ad Experiment foundational setup (Ch. 3):**
- [ ] Quickly build the landing pages.
- [ ] Create the new Business Manager.
- [ ] Set up tracking/pixel.
- [ ] Launch Campaign 1: a Like Ad (Page Likes) campaign with **3 different images** to build fans cheaply.
- [ ] Launch Campaign 2: a long-copy Link Post Ad Website Conversions campaign with **2 different images** to drive subscribers/conversions.
- [ ] Let Facebook find the winning creative within each campaign.

## Pitfalls to avoid

- **Don't combine many interests in one ad set** when you want to compare — you lose per-interest performance visibility. One (or two related) interests per ad set (Ch. 11).
- **Don't pick CPM for most advertisers** — it's brand-advertising-oriented (pay per view whether or not anyone clicks) and not the best bidding option for most goals (Fundamentals).
- **Don't expect to switch budget types in place** — daily vs. lifetime can't be changed after selection; you must duplicate the ad set (A.P.B. / Fundamentals).
- **Don't duplicate at the increased budget.** When duplicating a winning ad set, start the copy at the *original* starting budget, not the later-raised one — duplicating at the increased budget produces worse results. Example: original raised from $10/day to $20/day → duplicates should be $10/day, not $20/day (Five-Tier Scaling).
- **Don't run more than one ad per ad set** except for smaller audiences (rotate variations, one live at a time) — and never in the Michigan Method (Five-Tier Scaling).
- **Don't launch late in the day** — use a scheduled start to go live early the next morning instead (A.P.B.).
- **Don't ignore conflicting content** next to your ads — use exclude-categories and block lists on in-stream video, audience network, and instant articles (A.P.B.).
