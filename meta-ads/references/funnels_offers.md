# funnels_offers

> Proven Meta (Facebook/Instagram) funnel architectures, offer types, hooks, and lead-magnet strategy for moving cold prospects through awareness and intent into customers — from "Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)".

## Core frameworks

### Facebook Flight Plan
A perpetual system (the book's "aeronautical chart") that runs ads month after month — create the campaign once and let it run, targeting millions, generating thousands of leads/customers automatically while continuously growing the audience. It systematically moves people through three stages (Ch. 14–15):
1. Completely **unaware** of the problem the product solves.
2. Fully **aware** of the problem and having intent to solve it.
3. Having **intent to want to buy YOUR specific** product or service.

You are perpetually building awareness, building fans, generating leads/customers, and creating goodwill along the way. The book teaches building your own **FANS system** on top of this (Ch. 2).

### FANS system
The named (trademarked) perpetual machine the book helps you build on top of the Facebook Flight Plan: continuously building **A**wareness, **F**ans, leads/customers, and goodwill with your audience along the way (Ch. 2). The end goal is an automated customer-acquisition machine that transforms social users who are unaware of the problem and the brand into people aware of the problem, aware of the solution, and with intent to purchase — the structural reason for funnels rather than a single ad asking for the sale (Your Facebook Flight School).

### UPSYD (retargeting / awareness ladder)
The awareness-to-purchase ladder used in Stage Three retargeting (Ch. 13/15). Notable rung captured here:
- **D = Deal / buying incentive** — nudge hesitant hot prospects with a bonus, discount, or expiration/scarcity (see Tactics).
After an educational video ad, prospects can move from unawareness all the way up the UPSYD ladder.

### Six Proven Facebook-Friendly Offers
Six offer types proven across multiple industries (identified after **$27.7M** in agency ad spend), listed in **reverse order of commitment** (1 = least, 6 = most) (Ch. 17):
1. **Coupon, Sweepstakes, or Blog** — lowest commitment.
2. **Checklist, Swipe File, or Cheat Sheet Download.**
3. **Quiz / Survey Funnel.**
4. **Webinar or Event.**
5. **Free + Shipping for Physical Products.**
6. **Product Sales Page** — highest commitment.

Beginners launching a first campaign should start with **just ONE** offer to get initial momentum. Intermediate/advanced advertisers scaling may use several — but always match the right offer with the right ad type.

### Bow Tie Formula (Sophisticated vs Complicated Funnels)
BlitzMetrics' name for a remarketing-driven funnel strategy (Appendix — Dennis Yu):
- **Complicated** = tons of remarketing (custom), lookalike, and interest/behavior audiences spread across many ad sets.
- **Sophisticated** = most campaigns driven by **remarketing** instead of static demographic/interest targets.
A truly powerful funnel has many **sequences** (IF/THEN statements). More touches = more chances to build value, raising user LTV and letting you spend more to acquire leads.

### Value, Value, Value, Offer
Deliver value repeatedly before making an offer so the funnel generates goodwill and feels like a trusted friend's recommendation rather than selling (Appendix — Dennis Yu, per Michael Hunter's sequences for Brendon Burchard). Combined with "thinking like journalists," articles gain authority that earns placement on high-profile sites, amplified via switch-boosting to media-inception targets.

## Tactics & best practices

### Proven funnel flows
- **eCommerce Funnel 1 — Lead Magnet to Sales** (Ch. 12): Facebook **video** ad → basic squeeze-page lead magnet (e.g., one-day detox) → product sales page → order form. Video is used to get **quality** leads (brand-aware, warm) so the transition to sale is easier. After opt-in, a video acknowledges their last action, gives access directions, thanks them, guides them on using the offer, then transitions into why your product is the next best step.
- **eCommerce Funnel 2 — Direct to Sales** (Ch. 12): Facebook ad → **long-form** sales page → order form (no lead magnet — one step further down the funnel). With the right messaging (audience's frustration, challenge, deep desires), you can take a **cold** audience directly to a sales page. Long-form page = product info, testimonials (trust), a desire-building video, order-form buttons.
- **Info/Digital Funnel 1 — Lead Magnet + Tripwire + Core + Upsell** (Ch. 12): Facebook **link-post** ad offering a valuable lead magnet → lead-magnet opt-in → tripwire → core offer → upsell. Works in big B2C niches (fitness, passionate niches) and B2B.
- **Info/Digital Funnel 2 — Webinar / Live Event** (Ch. 12): Facebook **video** ad (bigger commitment) → webinar/live-event registration page → thank-you page. Ideal for **high-priced** products because you get more time with prospects.
- **Local/Retail/Service Funnels 1 & 2 — Discount/Incentive to Landing Page** (Ch. 12): A website-conversion campaign driving traffic to a landing page with a coupon or giveaway. The two versions are the same funnel — one uses a **video** ad, the other an **image-based link-post** ad (the recommended starting point: keep it simple). Coupon ideas: 7 days free at a gym, BOGO, free consultation. People share coupons, broadening reach.

### Offer-specific tactics (Ch. 17)
- **Offer #1 Coupon/Sweepstakes/Blog** — least commitment; coupon (e.g., "$30 off" / "$50 off") works wonders for local businesses. Deliver via **video ad → coupon landing page** OR **short-copy ad → coupon offer** (video NOT required; both work equally well). Example: repurposed a 30-second local TV spot as a Facebook video ad → coupon landing page (Eagle Leather) and it crushed it. Hidden benefit: organic brand exposure + subconscious brand-to-problem association.
- **Short-Copy Ad to Ungated Blog Post** — drive cold traffic to free "ungated" content (no opt-in) to indoctrinate the brand without pitching and feed a remarketing campaign. Long-term goodwill play; also works short-term. Success hinges on the right HOOK; requires proper tracking (e.g., Wicked Reports, Ch. 16) to identify and promote best-performing content. (Author's favorite campaign type.)
- **Offer #2 Checklist/Swipe File/Cheat Sheet** — short-copy ad → simple opt-in landing page to download a tool that gets a result. Example: "The 7-Step Facebook Campaign Checklist" (copy slightly longer because a webinar reg page follows). Digital Marketer's "The Ultimate Social Media Swipe File" runs as a **display** ad (not just newsfeed) and does incredibly well in both placements.
- **Offer #3 Quiz/Survey Funnel** — curiosity-driven hook via **link-post ad → quiz/survey landing page**. Works like a doctor's prescription: **branch logic**, tailored to the person, ends in an answer-based solution. **Opt-in required** to see results; prescription can be free or paid. Non-buyers still hand you data on the avatar's fears/desires for crafting hooks. Example hook: "How Old Is Your Metabolism?"
- **Offer #4 Webinar/Event** — higher commitment (asking 90–120 minutes), so **pre-commit with long copy**. Proven method: 30-second clip with a text-overlay hook, then elaborate in long copy. Copy method: restate the hook → make them aware of a problem → deliver on the hook's promise (e.g., reveal "the three biggest mistakes") → invite to register for the free web class. Each step **seeds** the next (read → register → show up). Example: "Game Changing Facebook Changes."
- **Offer #5 Free + Shipping (physical)** — customer pays only shipping; lowers barrier for cold audiences and converts prospects into qualified buyers, justified by a backend value ladder. Method (book free+shipping, 2nd ed.): a video taught the #1 lesson from $600k/month avg ad spend, delivered the hook by teaching the four targeting groups to start every campaign with (elevating prospect status), then invited them to buy the book. Video ~10 minutes; content delivering on the hook matters more than length. Economics: they pay for the leads (book bought from publisher) but it makes sense given the value-ladder ascension.
- **Offer #6 Product Sales Page (educational video ad)** — most powerful ad type. To cold traffic, give value upfront — never run a straight CTA-to-buy video ("there has to be a whole lot more to it than just a CTA"). EDUCATE to build authority/credibility/rapport. Example: Drew Canole's "11 Superfoods" video educates, then **pivots** to offer an easier all-in-one solution (Organifi green juice) → long-form sales page (top-of-fold hook restates benefits; page restates the video). Implement the three-step video ad formula (Ch. 22).

### Hook & offer engineering (Ch. 16)
- **Splintering Your Offer** — splinter off an easily consumable, highly actionable, highly valuable, popular chunk of your main product as the front-end offer for cold traffic. (Guitar example: a 7-lesson "How to learn rock guitar" course → most-popular riffs lesson → 5 killer licks; splinter one piece off.) Give away your best stuff, but keep it **incomplete** so they want more.
- **Ad-to-Landing-Page Congruency (Ad Scent)** — match the ad copy, headline, and even color scheme to the hook; congruency from ad to landing page is essential for conversions (e.g., landing-page headline matches "When Is the Best Time to Send Your Emails?").
- **Ad Scent** — the visual/message continuity between an ad and its landing page (imagery, headline, message). Weak ad scent kills conversions even with strong clicks; easy to overlook, easy to fix — check that the ad and landing page clearly belong together (Troubleshooting).

### Retargeting tactics (Ch. 15)
- **Buying incentive (the "D" in UPSYD)** — nudge hot prospects with a bonus, discount, or expiration/scarcity. (See copy templates.)
- **Cross-sell / bundle retargeting** — for ecom stores with multiple products, retarget hot prospects with a similar/complementary product they may not know about.

### Lead-quality & thank-you-page tactics (Ch. 12)
- **Use a thank-you page offer to sift low-quality giveaway leads** — for free giveaways most leads are low quality, so put a coupon/offer on the thank-you page to separate freebie-seekers from genuine prospects and give them an incentive to call or come in.
- **Pick one best product, keep it simple to start** — even with 150+ products, don't build a page per product; run one of the two proven ecom funnels on your single top seller, messaging the audience's ultimate desire or biggest frustration. Add retargeting and dynamic product ads later ("icing on the cake").
- **Webinar funnel for high-ticket cold traffic** — use webinars to warm cold traffic for **$500+** products by delivering value over a longer window, then transition to the offer. Registration is only part of the battle: they must also show up AND actually consume the content.

### Other tactics
- **Work Backward From the End Goal to the Hook** (Ch. 16) — define the ultimate offer/end goal first, then work backward through the offer to the hook. Each step (hook → free offer → paid offer) must be a logical, **incomplete** progression toward the core product (typically what the prospect NEEDS, not just what they want).
- **Boost third-party publications over your own site** (Appendix) — boosting earned guest articles/quotes/mentions is usually more powerful than boosting your own site; OK to send ad dollars to someone else's site if the content is good. Place each article where it fits (e.g., Stannals, Influencive) and promote from your public-figure page.
- **Switch-boost to remarketing audiences for funnel conversion** (Appendix) — set triggers at conversion parts of the funnel to switch-boost authority content to remarketing audiences; for B2B or long consideration phases, surfacing high-authority mentions to remarketing lifts conversion by overcoming objections.
- **Leverage Facebook Live & Facebook Login** (Ch. 1) — use Facebook Live and Facebook Login to capture attention, collect contact info, and drive calls, registrations, and donations at the touch of a button.
- **Landing pages & allowed click destinations** (A Few Fundamentals) — where users land depends on ad type; you can send users anywhere not violating Facebook's landing-page policies (your own page, or on-platform: Page, Messenger, event, group). The advertiser specifies the landing-page URL when creating the ad.

## Checklists

### Find a Solution / craft the hook & offer (Ch. 16)
- [ ] Review your notes on the market's desires and pain points (the logical starting point).
- [ ] Force-rank those pain points and desires.
- [ ] Separate **wants** from **needs** — they are not the same.
- [ ] Devise solutions your **hook** will promise and your **offer** will fulfill.
- [ ] Ensure the offer fulfills the hook's promise but stays **INCOMPLETE**, so the bigger solution lives in the paid product.
- [ ] Work backward: define the end-goal/core offer first, then offer, then hook.
- [ ] Confirm each step (hook → free offer → paid offer) is a logical, incomplete progression toward the core product.

### Ad-to-landing-page congruency check (Ch. 16 / Troubleshooting)
- [ ] Landing-page headline matches the ad's hook.
- [ ] Ad copy and landing-page copy are aligned ("ad scent").
- [ ] Color schemes match to reinforce congruency.
- [ ] Ad and landing page clearly look like they belong together.

### Always use a thank-you page (Ch. 12)
- [ ] Build a thank-you page (Facebook no longer keeps coupon redemption on-platform).
- [ ] For giveaways, place a coupon/offer on the thank-you page to filter lead quality.

## Copy/templates

Low-friction lead magnet / tripwire (James, Ch. 4) — text-to-opt-in trigger → cheap high-value next step → benefit-driven promise:

```
And check this out—if you text "guitar master" to 555-555-play,
I'll send you a cool "Five-day guitar boot camp for $20" coupon
that one of my best instructors puts on every week. He shows you
even more tips, tricks, and secrets to getting great at the guitar fast.
```

eCommerce Funnel 1 — lead-magnet-to-sale transition (Ch. 12):

```
[Hook with the one-day detox's promise to jumpstart weight loss, then
transition to the sale:]
...imagine having 11 amazing super foods in a great tasting, convenient,
on-the-go powder that you can take every day.
```

Buying-incentive retargeting — the "D" in UPSYD (Ch. 15):

```
Get a 40 percent discount if you purchase by tomorrow!
```

```
Get a free tote bag with purchase of any performance gear!
(Value $25. Hurry—offer ends soon!)
```

Hook revision — house-flipping case study (Ch. 16):

```
Old (gives everything away): "How to flip houses in five simple steps"
  → 70-page eBook lead magnet → thousands of cheap leads, ZERO buyers.

New (incomplete, sells the need): "17 words you need to say to an investor
  to fund your next deal" → 3-page checklist → far more purchases.
```

Quiz/survey funnel hook example (Ch. 17): `How Old Is Your Metabolism?`

Webinar offer example (Ch. 17): `Game Changing Facebook Changes` (deliver "the three biggest mistakes," then invite to a free web class)

## Pitfalls to avoid

- **Don't give away the whole solution in the lead magnet (Incompleteness Principle)** (Ch. 16) — a "how to flip houses in five simple steps" 70-page eBook generated thousands of cheap leads but **zero buyers** because the paid 8-module course taught the same thing. The offer must fulfill the hook's promise yet stay **incomplete**, so the paid product delivers the bigger solution. The goal of a lead isn't a lead — it's a buyer. (Exception: it would have worked if splintered as one hook among a full portfolio of other products.)
- **"Give me your business card" = the polite no** (Ch. 4) — a high-friction, you-centric pitch that ends with "give me your card and I'll check it out" is a soft rejection, not a lead. If the funnel relies on the prospect remembering to find you later, you've lost them — engineer immediate, easy action at peak interest.
- **Don't send cold traffic straight to a catalog or bare coupon** (Ch. 12) — running a regular link-post ad to all products / a coupon with no rapport, interest, or desire messaging fails. Raise awareness of the audience's frustration, challenge, and deep desires first.
- **Don't run a straight CTA-to-buy video to cold traffic** (Ch. 17) — "it won't work; there has to be a whole lot more to it than just a CTA." Educate and deliver value upfront.
- **Facebook on-platform coupons are no longer allowed** (Ch. 12) — Facebook used to keep coupon redemption on-platform with no thank-you page; that's gone, so you must build a thank-you page (also essential for filtering giveaway lead quality).
- **Most free-giveaway leads are low quality** (Ch. 12) — without a thank-you-page offer to sift them, you collect freebie-seekers, not prospects.
- **Webinar registration is only half the battle** (Flight Plan) — even a free webinar is a big ask; your best prospects value time as much as money. They must register, show up, AND actually consume the content.
- **Confusing sophisticated with complicated** (Appendix) — piling on tons of demographic/interest ad sets is complicated, not sophisticated. Sophistication comes from remarketing-driven sequences (the Bow Tie Formula).
