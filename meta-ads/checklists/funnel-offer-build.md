# Funnel & Offer Build Checklist

> Craft the hook + offer and pick a proven funnel architecture before building the campaign. Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*, Ch. 12 / 16 / 17.

---

## 1. Find a Solution / craft the hook & offer (Ch. 16)

- [ ] Review your notes on the market's desires and pain points
- [ ] Force-rank those pain points and desires
- [ ] Separate **wants** from **needs** (they are not the same)
- [ ] Devise solutions your **hook** will promise and your **offer** will fulfill
- [ ] Ensure the offer fulfills the hook's promise but stays **INCOMPLETE** (the bigger solution lives in the paid product)
- [ ] Work backward: define the end-goal/core offer first, then offer, then hook
- [ ] Confirm each step (hook → free offer → paid offer) is a logical, incomplete progression toward the core product

## 2. Move Toward Desire — 3 desire questions (Ch. 16)

- [ ] What are our prospect's desires?
- [ ] What does our prospect want more than anything else?
- [ ] What do we have in our product that moves them toward that one thing they want most?
      (Then repeatedly ask "why do they really want this?" → desire behind the desire)

## 3. Move Away From Pain — 3 pain questions (Ch. 16)

- [ ] What are your target market's greatest problems? (What keeps them up at night?)
- [ ] What are they most afraid of?
- [ ] Why do they want to change? (Then embed your solution in the hook and cure it with your offer)

## 4. A great offer is (Ch. 16)

- [ ] Highly specific
- [ ] Easily consumable
- [ ] Provides immediate results when implemented
- [ ] Speaks to a known desire
- [ ] Incomplete relative to the end product

## 5. Pick a proven offer — Six Proven Facebook-Friendly Offers (Ch. 17)

> Listed in reverse order of commitment (1 = least, 6 = most). Beginners: start with just ONE.

- [ ] #1 Coupon, Sweepstakes, or Blog (lowest commitment)
- [ ] #2 Checklist, Swipe File, or Cheat Sheet Download
- [ ] #3 Quiz / Survey Funnel
- [ ] #4 Webinar or Event
- [ ] #5 Free + Shipping for Physical Products
- [ ] #6 Product Sales Page (highest commitment)

## 6. Pick a proven funnel flow (Ch. 12)

- [ ] eCommerce 1 — Video ad → lead-magnet squeeze page → product sales page → order form
- [ ] eCommerce 2 — Ad → long-form sales page → order form (no lead magnet)
- [ ] Info/Digital 1 — Link-post ad → lead-magnet opt-in → tripwire → core offer → upsell
- [ ] Info/Digital 2 — Video ad → webinar/live-event registration → thank-you page (high-ticket)
- [ ] Local/Retail/Service — Website-conversion campaign → landing page with coupon/giveaway (start simple: image-based link-post ad)

## 7. Ad-to-landing-page congruency / Ad Scent (Ch. 16 / Troubleshooting)

- [ ] Landing-page headline matches the ad's hook
- [ ] Ad copy and landing-page copy are aligned
- [ ] Color schemes match
- [ ] Ad and landing page clearly look like they belong together

## 8. Always use a thank-you page (Ch. 12)

- [ ] Build a thank-you page (Facebook no longer keeps coupon redemption on-platform)
- [ ] For giveaways, place a coupon/offer on the thank-you page to filter lead quality

---

## Pitfalls to avoid

- [ ] Don't give away the whole solution in the lead magnet (Incompleteness Principle)
- [ ] Don't send cold traffic straight to a catalog or bare coupon
- [ ] Don't run a straight CTA-to-buy video to cold traffic — educate first
- [ ] Remember: most free-giveaway leads are low quality without a thank-you-page filter
- [ ] Webinar registration is only half the battle — they must register, show up, AND consume

## Key numbers / thresholds

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Landing-page conversion rate (average) | ~20% |
| Landing-page conversion rate (problem level) | < 10% |
| Six proven offers — ad spend basis | $27.7M |
| Tripwire price | usually < $20 |
| Webinar time ask | 90–120 minutes |
| High-ticket webinar threshold | $500+ |
| Free+Shipping video example length | ~10 minutes |
