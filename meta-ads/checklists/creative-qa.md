# Creative QA Checklist

> Image and video creative QA for Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ads — the 6-step designer process, single-image build steps, the Three-Step Video Ad Formula, and creative red-flags. Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*, Ch. 21 / 28 / Three-Step Video Ad Formula.

---

## 1. Six Important Aspects your creative should have (Ch. 21)

> Not all must appear in every creative, but **#1 always must**.

- [ ] **Reflect your hook** (MANDATORY)
- [ ] Tell a story about how your offer changes their life (carousel ads are great for this)
- [ ] Display the product (if physical/demonstrable; works best further down funnel)
- [ ] Stand out in the newsfeed (contrasting colors, small video / moving GIFs — flashy but on brand)
- [ ] Be on brand (consistent fonts and colors)
- [ ] Play off emotions already associated with an image (e.g., low iPhone battery, low bank account)

## 2. Ad Creative Checklist — 6-step designer thought process (Ch. 21)

- [ ] **Create a powerful message and conceptualize images** — research the hook, Google, competitors; figure out benefits, target, end result, emotions to convey
- [ ] **Sketch out ideas** before laying out design elements — don't worry about colors/fonts yet; ensure all marketing elements are present
- [ ] **Use complementary or contrasting colors** — only a few choices (not ten — it gets busy)
- [ ] **Choose your fonts** — only one or two font families for readability
- [ ] **Add text and a CTA to your image** — put a few of the most important hook words (a line of copy or a button) on it
- [ ] **Make the image congruent with your brand** — landing-page/destination design should match the ad

## 3. Steps to build a Single Image ad (Ch. 28)

- [ ] After creating your ad set, click **Continue** to reach the ad creation page
- [ ] In **Format**, choose one of the five formats (Single Image for this walkthrough)
- [ ] Under **Images**, upload new / reuse / use free stock (follow Recommended Image Specs)
- [ ] Under **Page and Links**, select your Facebook page; add an Instagram account if you have one
- [ ] Enter your **Website URL** and ad copy
- [ ] Add a **Call to Action** button
- [ ] Click **Review Order**
- [ ] Click the green **Place Order** button (Facebook reviews campaign, ad set, ad)
- [ ] Click the blue **Continue** button to finish
- [ ] After confirmation, create a new ad in the same window to test different images or copy

## 4. Before you publish a VIDEO ad (Three-Step Video Ad Formula)

- [ ] First 3–10 seconds stop the scroll
- [ ] At least one EDIE element present (Educate, Demonstrate, Inform, or Entertain)
- [ ] Audio is strong/memorable (assume sound-on autoplay)
- [ ] Avatar is called out directly
- [ ] A pain point is called out in a Policy-compliant way
- [ ] The customer's authentic voice / individuality comes through (ideally customer appears on camera)
- [ ] Educates and delivers upfront value BEFORE pivoting to sell (for cold traffic)
- [ ] A portal (visual/auditory) is used to reset attention and prime the decision
- [ ] At least one clear CTA — and multiple CTAs across a long video
- [ ] On videos ~3 min+, a soft CTA appears within the first 60–90 seconds
- [ ] CTA(s) are authentic to the brand, "seeding the next thing" (not a hard "buy now" infomercial)
- [ ] Video length matches the brand/product (no arbitrary length rule)
- [ ] Checked the client's existing YouTube channel for reusable footage

## 5. Working backward from the offer — hook design (video)

- [ ] Define the end business goal (e.g., get prospects on a call / to purchase)
- [ ] Identify the offer's "missing ingredient" the video pre-frames
- [ ] Derive the hook from the offer

## 6. Creative red-flags (must all be clear)

- [ ] No eyeball-bait imagery (puppy / pretty woman) unrelated to the hook
- [ ] No lazy random stock image (e.g., the stock cat at a keyboard)
- [ ] Custom image still has marketing thought (a graph that matches the copy, not a meaningless one)
- [ ] Not photographic stock that blends into friends'/family's photos — prefer designed/branded imagery
- [ ] Not too many fonts (limit to one or two families)
- [ ] Not too many colors (a few complementary/contrasting only)
- [ ] If people appear, their gaze points at the viewer or the CTA
- [ ] Don't rely on Facebook's auto-caption generator (regional accents cause errors — control captions yourself)
- [ ] Don't run a straight "buy now" CTA video to cold traffic — educate/give value first

## Key numbers / thresholds

| Metric | Value / threshold |
|---|---|
| Viewer attention per image | 2–3 seconds |
| Recommended Like-ad image size | 1200 × 444 px |
| Standard/familiar image size | 1200 × 628 px |
| Ad format options | 5 (Carousel, Single Image, Single Video, Slideshow, Canvas) |
| Image sourcing options | 3 (upload new, reuse previous, free stock) |
| Image ideas per hook (Ad Grid) | ~3–5 |
| Typical creative revisions | 3–5 (sometimes 1) |
| Video stop-the-scroll window | 3–10 seconds |
| Soft CTA timing (videos ~3 min+) | within first 60–90 sec |
| First CTA placement (9-min video) | ~90 seconds in |
| CTAs in a 9-min video | ~4 total |
| Best-performer video length range | 20 sec – 18 min |
