# Ad Copy QA Checklist

> Copywriting QA for Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ads — the 7 Power Questions, persuasion elements, hook design, awareness-matching, and Policy compliance. Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*, Ch. 16 / 18 / 20 + The 13 Elements.

---

## 1. Pre-publish copy check — 7 Power Questions (Ryan Deiss, Ch. 20)

> Single underlying goal: FOCUSED ATTENTION. Answering even one or two well beats most competitors.

- [ ] Does the offer appear novel, unique, and distinctive?
- [ ] Is the offer simple and easy to understand?
- [ ] Is there an opening question that triggers consistency/action?
- [ ] Have I pre-exposed the audience to a desired emotional concept?
- [ ] Have I tapped mental links/associations (memory, nostalgia, identity)?
- [ ] Have I opened a loop to hold attention until the close?
- [ ] Have I created a mental/visual portal to new opportunity?

## 2. The 7 Ad Building Blocks present where needed (Ch. 18)

> Not every ad needs all seven; mix-and-match Lego-style.

- [ ] Curiosity
- [ ] Challenge / Frustration / Pain or Desire
- [ ] Benefits and Desires (append "so you can" / "so that")
- [ ] Credibility or Authority Statement
- [ ] An "Aha Moment"
- [ ] Call-to-Action (CTA) — crystal clear
- [ ] Overcoming Objections (mini-FAQ after the first CTA)

## 3. Hook design QA (Ch. 16)

- [ ] Hook ≠ offer — the creative conveys the **hook**, not just the product
- [ ] Hook is specific to a narrow avatar with the solution embedded in the problem it agitates
- [ ] First line of post copy opens with a question addressing the hook
- [ ] Headline restates that question as a benefit statement (same theme, two ways, two places)
- [ ] Hook matches the audience's UPSYD awareness level (don't lead with the solution name to broad audiences)

## 4. A great offer is (Ch. 16)

- [ ] Highly specific
- [ ] Easily consumable
- [ ] Provides immediate results when implemented
- [ ] Speaks to a known desire
- [ ] Incomplete relative to the end product (leaves them wanting the paid product)

## 5. Persuasion polish — 13 Elements (use sparingly) (Ralph Burns)

- [ ] Authentic voice (don't copy verbatim)
- [ ] Simplify — one-syllable words, ~5th-grade reading level ("Write for Homer")
- [ ] Show Proof — credibility statements tied to pain points
- [ ] Ask Questions / Answer Immediately
- [ ] Use Revealing Words (Discover, hidden, astonishing) — NOT hyperbole; action verbs : adjectives ≥ 2:1
- [ ] "You" rules — second person, but NEVER tied to a personal attribute (Policy)
- [ ] Non-Standard Callouts — brackets/parentheses, e.g., `[Special Discount]`
- [ ] Capitalize only the ONE word/phrase to remember (never all-caps everything)
- [ ] Embrace the Ellipsis to create a broken thought
- [ ] Mix It Up — split-test 3–4 copy lengths
- [ ] Employ Emojis (don't overdo) — split tests show ↑ CTR, ↑ Relevance Score, ↓ CPC, ↓ CPA

## 6. Copy length — split-test (Mix It Up)

- [ ] Ultra-short: 1–2 lines
- [ ] Short: ~3–5 lines (pairs with strong video)
- [ ] Long: 5+ lines (image ads selling a core product/consult; cold-traffic big asks)
- [ ] Long-ass: up to 1,000+ words (heavy persuasion, big cold-traffic asks)

## 7. Testimonial-gathering script — 5 questions (Ch. 15)

- [ ] What problem/pain/frustration were they trying to solve before your product?
- [ ] What were they worried/hesitant about before buying? (overcomes objections)
- [ ] What were their results? How is life/business better now? (transformation)
- [ ] What did they like best about [your product]?
- [ ] Would they recommend it to a friend? If so, how can it help them?

## 8. Policy & quality red-flags (must all be clear)

- [ ] No direct claims/promises about the audience (e.g., "Lose 30 lbs in 30 Days" is banned — share your OWN results instead)
- [ ] "You" is never tied to a personal attribute ("Do you have diabetes?", "Do you want to lose weight?" violate Policy)
- [ ] No all-caps everything (scammy; Policy notices) — capitalize only the one key thing
- [ ] No exaggeration / hyperbole / superlatives (best, finest, outstanding)
- [ ] No subjective income-claim hooks (e.g., "make $30,000 in 30 days", "six-figure income as a coach")
- [ ] The next step is crystal clear — include a URL in the copy if needed
- [ ] Benefit-driven statements reviewed against Ad Policy
- [ ] Always use the Call-to-Action button (not just a URL)
- [ ] Story doesn't close too early (weave the message mid-story; close at the point of sale — Zeigarnik)

## Key numbers / thresholds

| Metric | Value / threshold |
|---|---|
| Like-ad copy limit | 90 characters (body only; headlines not editable) |
| Action verbs : adjectives | ≥ 2:1 |
| Soft CTA timing (video ~3 min+) | within first 60–90 sec |
| Qualified-lead long-copy threshold | ~1,000 words (Keto ad example) |
| Target reading level | ~5th grade |
| Copy lengths to split-test | 3–4 |
| US adult literacy | 50% can't read at 8th-grade level |
