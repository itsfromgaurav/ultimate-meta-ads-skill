# Template — Hook Formulas (UPSYD awareness + fill-in hook structures)

> Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*, Ch. 13 & 16.
> A **hook is your marketing message** — the front-facing message prospects see before becoming customers. Hook ≠ Offer: the offer is the action you want; the hook is how you sell/market it. **One offer can have ten hooks.** A great Facebook ad always starts with a great hook.

---

## UPSYD — Awareness Staircase (match every hook to where the prospect sits)
A five-step awareness continuum (a Facebook-friendly reversal of Eugene Schwartz's five levels of product awareness):

| Step | Level | State | How to message |
|---|---|---|---|
| **U** | Unaware | Doesn't realize they have a problem | Long-copy; make them aware of the problem, then transition to your solution |
| **P** | Problem Aware | Knows the problem, not the solution | Long-copy; agitate problem → introduce solution |
| **S** | Solution Aware | Knows solutions exist (low-hanging fruit) | Simpler short-copy referencing the known problem |
| **Y** | Your-Solution Aware | Knows your specific product, hasn't bought | Direct; reference your product by name |
| **D** | Deal | Ready to buy on an offer | Buying incentive: bonus, discount, expiration/scarcity |

**Key tradeoff:** the higher you start, the more directly you can go for the sale — but the audience is smaller. The lower you start, the more value/education you must provide — but it scales far better. **Default start: Level 3 (Solution Aware).** Goal of every ad: move people UP the staircase.

⚠️ Don't lead with the solution NAME to broad audiences (e.g., "Download your FREE Intermittent Fasting cheat sheet" only lands on Your-Solution-Aware people; "fasting" may even repel the unaware). Use a more specific or curiosity-driven hook to reach broad/unaware markets.

## Fill-in hook formulas

### 1. Desire + Benefit Hook Formula
```
They want [specific desire] so that they can [benefit].
```
> [specific desire] = what they want more than anything else; [benefit] = the end benefit of fulfilling it. Write out about a DOZEN of these and keep going until you find the perfect match.

### 2. If/Then Hook Statement
```
If only [solution]... Then [benefit].
```
> The "if" reveals the solution (or desire/pain); the "then" reveals the benefit. Make sure the solution you agitate is embedded in your own offer.
> Example: "If only there was a reliable system that would simplify all the setup and analysis. Then that would allow me to take on more customers." → derived hook: "how to reliably track your Facebook leads, sales, and ROI for your customers."

### 3. Question-first-line → Benefit-headline pattern
```
First line of post copy: [Looking for an easy way to get more superfoods?]
Headline:                [Get all your healthy superfoods in one drink.]
```
> Same theme, two ways, two places. The first line of post copy is the most important line in the ad.

### 4. Knowledge-Gap / Curiosity hook
```
[Counter-intuitive question that creates an itch to scratch]
```
> Example: "Is breakfast REALLY the most important meal of the day?" Best when combined with a counter-intuitive statement.

### 5. Big Aha Moment hook (contrarian + valuable)
```
Is [conventional belief] really true, or is it actually [contrarian reframe]?
```
> Example: "Is breakfast really the most important meal of the day, or is it the most important meal that should be skipped?"

### 6. Specificity + Immediacy hook
```
How to [super-specific, immediate, measurable result] (not a broad/long-term one)
```
> "how to sell from the stage" beats "build a six-figure coaching practice." "drive the ball ten yards farther" beats vague benefits. Find the one tiny thing that compels the click.

### 7. Novelty hook
```
Major announcement
```
> Signals the new/unfamiliar (survival instinct). TV equivalent: "Breaking News."

### 8. Identity / Commitment-Consistency opener (Q3)
```
Pardon me — do you consider yourself to be a [positive identity trait, e.g., helpful] person?
Great! If so, [tie that self-image to the desired action]...
```

## "Move Toward Desire" — 3 desire questions
- [ ] What are our prospect's desires?
- [ ] What does our prospect want more than anything else?
- [ ] What do we have in our product that moves them toward that one thing they want most?
→ Then repeatedly ask "**why do they really want this?**" to reach the *desire behind the desire*.

## "Move Away From Pain" — 3 pain questions (loss aversion ~2× gains)
- [ ] What are your target market's greatest problems? (What keeps them up at night?)
- [ ] What are they most afraid of?
- [ ] Why do they want to change?
→ Then embed your solution in the hook and cure it with your offer.

---

## "Why the hook works" — 4-criteria check (Ketogenic Living example)
- [ ] Curiosity-based and contrarian.
- [ ] Shatters a conventional myth held by millions.
- [ ] Educates/informs on something new and interesting.
- [ ] Speaks to the avatar's pain point.

## "A great offer is" checklist (the bait the hook sells)
- [ ] Highly specific.
- [ ] Easily consumable.
- [ ] Provides immediate results when implemented.
- [ ] Speaks to a known desire.
- [ ] Incomplete relative to the end product (leaves them wanting the paid product).

## STEPPS — Six Principles of Sharing (build into hooks to maximize shares)
- **S** Social Currency — sharing makes them look good.
- **T** Triggers — top of mind = tip of tongue.
- **E** Emotions — when we care, we share.
- **P** Public Visibility — built to show = built to grow.
- **P** Practical Value — something they can actually use.
- **S** Stories — info shared in an entertaining manner.
