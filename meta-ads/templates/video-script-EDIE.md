# Template — Video Script: The Three-Step Video Ad Formula (Stop the Scroll → EDIE → CTA)

> Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)* — Dominate Web Media. Backed by 600M+ paid video views.
> The formula is flexible: there is NO fixed required length. Best performers ranged from **20 seconds to 18 minutes**. Match content to your brand and product; everything else is fluid.

---

## STEP 1 — Stop the Scroll (first 3–10 seconds)
Grab attention before the audio fades. Use at least one proven tactic:
- [ ] **Blatant hand motions** — wave at the viewer, pull them toward you.
- [ ] **Text overlay / captions** — catches sound-off viewers; delivers the message faster (we read faster than we speak).
- [ ] **Pattern interrupt** — make the picture look unusual (movement, extreme close-up, selfie-style).


## STEP 2 — EDIE Formula (include AT LEAST ONE, not all four)
EDIE = **E**ducate, **D**emonstrate, **I**nform, or **E**ntertain — ensures at least one of Berger's virality principles is present so the video is watched, enjoyed, remembered, and shared.

- **Educate** — teach/give tips (you don't even have to be on camera; a screen recording works). Don't give a full webinar; give value + at least one "Aha!" nugget that reveals the value of your offer, then say they'll find more after the CTA.
- **Demonstrate** — show the product in action (great for tech brands). Showing it work makes viewers want their own.
- **Inform** — give info the viewer didn't know before (when the product needs little education but has features people should know).
- **Entertain** — hardest to do well (e.g., Dollar Beard Club parody of a Dollar Shave Club video).

## STEP 3 — Call to Action (often multiple times)
Tell the viewer exactly what to do next. Keep it natural and authentic — "a marathon, not a late-night buy-now-while-stocks-last infomercial." Always seed the next step.
- On videos ~3 min+, place a **soft CTA within the first 60–90 seconds**.
- A 9-minute video should have ~4 CTAs total (only a portion watch to the end).

## Authentic Video Formula (avatar + pain point + personality)
> "The exact formula we use for nearly all our videos." The copy doesn't have to pose a question — it can simply get the prospect thinking about a shared problem, then instruct them what to do next.
1. Call out your **avatar** directly.
2. Call out a **pain point** in a Policy-compliant way.
3. Express the customer's **individuality / authentic voice**.
4. **Reinforce all of it with the video itself** — ideally the customer appears IN the video as the voice of the business.

```
Avatar + pain-point opener:
"[Get the prospect thinking about a problem they share], then tell them exactly
what to do next: Watch the video, then click the link for more tips."
```

## Portal (reset attention, prime the decision)
Move the prospect from one place/state to another so they're open to change.
```
Plumber walk-through portal:
"Hi, I'm [name]... come inside, I want you to meet some people."
(camera follows through the door into the store)
```
Options: visual (drive-along), walk-through, auditory (start/change background music), or subtle (background color change).

## Pre-publish video checklist
- [ ] First 3–10 seconds stop the scroll
- [ ] At least one EDIE element present
- [ ] Audio is strong/memorable (assume sound-on autoplay; audio > video)
- [ ] Avatar called out directly
- [ ] A pain point called out in a Policy-compliant way
- [ ] Customer's authentic voice comes through (ideally on camera)
- [ ] Educates / delivers upfront value BEFORE pivoting to sell (cold traffic)
- [ ] A portal (visual/auditory) resets attention and primes the decision
- [ ] At least one clear CTA — multiple across a long video
- [ ] On videos ~3 min+, a soft CTA within the first 60–90 sec
- [ ] CTA(s) authentic to the brand, "seeding the next thing"
- [ ] Video length matches the brand/product (no arbitrary length rule)
- [ ] Checked the client's existing YouTube channel for reusable footage

## Working backward from the offer (hook design)
- [ ] Define the end business goal (get on a call / purchase)
- [ ] Identify the offer's "missing ingredient" the video pre-frames
- [ ] Derive the hook from the offer (e.g., hook "buy a house with no money" → checklist offer "15 things to flip your first house," one of which is you need funding)

## Pitfalls
- Don't run a video that's just a straight "buy now" CTA to cold traffic — "it won't work." Educate first, then pivot.
- Don't bury the CTA only at the end. Don't obey arbitrary length rules.
- Don't over-produce testimonials (too polished reads as less trustworthy than something shot on an iPhone).
- Don't skip the portal.
