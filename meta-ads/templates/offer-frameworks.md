# Template — The 6 Proven Facebook-Friendly Offers

> Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*, Ch. 17.
> Six offer types proven across multiple industries (identified after **$27.7M** in agency ad spend), listed in **reverse order of commitment** (1 = least, 6 = most).
> **Beginners: start with just ONE offer** to get initial momentum. Always match the right offer with the right ad type.

---

## Offer #1 — Coupon, Sweepstakes, or Blog (lowest commitment)
- **Delivery:** video ad → coupon landing page OR short-copy ad → coupon offer (video NOT required; both work).
- **Coupon ideas:** "$30 off" / "$50 off", 7 days free at a gym, BOGO, free consultation. People SHARE coupons → broader reach.
- **Variant — Short-Copy Ad → Ungated Blog Post:** drive cold traffic to free "ungated" content (no opt-in) to indoctrinate the brand and feed a remarketing campaign. Success hinges on the right HOOK.
```
Coupon ad fill-in:
[Hook] → "[$__ off / free __]" → [coupon landing page] → thank-you page
```

## Offer #2 — Checklist, Swipe File, or Cheat Sheet Download
- **Delivery:** short-copy ad → simple opt-in landing page to download a tool that gets a result.
```
Fill-in: "The [#]-Step [topic] Checklist/Cheat Sheet"
Example: "The 7-Step Facebook Campaign Checklist"
```

## Offer #3 — Quiz / Survey Funnel
- **Delivery:** curiosity-driven hook → link-post ad → quiz/survey landing page.
- Works like a doctor's prescription: **branch logic**, tailored to the person, ends in an answer-based solution. **Opt-in required** to see results. Non-buyers still hand you data on the avatar's fears/desires.
```
Fill-in hook: "How [adjective] Is Your [thing]?"
Example: "How Old Is Your Metabolism?"
```

## Offer #4 — Webinar or Event
- Higher commitment (asking 90–120 minutes), so **pre-commit with LONG copy**. Proven method: 30-second clip with a text-overlay hook, then elaborate in long copy.
- **Copy method:** restate the hook → make them aware of a problem → deliver on the hook's promise (e.g., reveal "the three biggest mistakes") → invite to register for the free web class. Each step SEEDS the next.
```
Fill-in:
[Restate hook] → [problem awareness] → "the [3] biggest mistakes [avatar] makes
with [topic]" → "Register for the free web class."
Example hook: "Game Changing Facebook Changes"
```

## Offer #5 — Free + Shipping (physical products)
- Customer pays only shipping; lowers barrier for cold audiences and converts prospects into qualified BUYERS, justified by a backend value ladder.
- **Method:** a video teaches the #1 lesson, delivers the hook (e.g., the four targeting groups to start every campaign with — elevating prospect status), then invites them to buy. Video ~10 minutes; content delivering on the hook matters more than length.
```
Fill-in:
[Educational video delivering the hook's #1 lesson] → "Just pay shipping" →
order form → backend value ladder
```

## Offer #6 — Product Sales Page (educational video ad) (highest commitment / most powerful)
- To COLD traffic, give value UPFRONT — **never run a straight CTA-to-buy video**. EDUCATE to build authority/credibility/rapport, then **pivot** to offer an easier all-in-one solution → long-form sales page.
- Example: Drew Canole's "11 Superfoods" video educates, then pivots to Organifi green juice → long-form sales page (top-of-fold hook restates benefits; page restates the video). Implement the Three-Step Video Ad Formula.
```
Fill-in:
[Educate: deliver real value + Aha moment] → [pivot: "there's an easier
all-in-one way..."] → [long-form sales page; top-of-fold hook restates benefits]
```

## Hook & offer engineering

### Splintering Your Offer
Splinter off an easily consumable, highly actionable, highly valuable, popular chunk of your main product as the front-end offer for cold traffic. Give away your best stuff, but keep it **INCOMPLETE** so they want more.
```
Main product: [____] → splinter the most popular piece: [____] → front-end offer: [____]
```

### Work Backward From the End Goal to the Hook
```
[Define ultimate offer / core product] ← [paid offer] ← [free offer] ← [hook]
Each step must be a logical, INCOMPLETE progression toward the core product.
```

### Hook revision — incompleteness in action
```
Old (gives everything away): "How to flip houses in five simple steps"
  → 70-page eBook → thousands of cheap leads, ZERO buyers.
New (incomplete, sells the need): "17 words you need to say to an investor
  to fund your next deal" → 3-page checklist → far more purchases.
```

### Ad-to-Landing-Page Congruency (Ad Scent) check
- [ ] Landing-page headline matches the ad's hook.
- [ ] Ad copy and landing-page copy are aligned.
- [ ] Color schemes match.
- [ ] Ad and landing page clearly look like they belong together.

### Buying-incentive retargeting ("D" in UPSYD)
```
"Get a 40 percent discount if you purchase by tomorrow!"
"Get a free tote bag with purchase of any performance gear! (Value $25. Hurry—offer ends soon!)"
```

## Proven funnel flows (by business category)
| Funnel | Flow |
|---|---|
| eCom 1 — Lead Magnet to Sales | Video ad → squeeze-page lead magnet → product sales page → order form |
| eCom 2 — Direct to Sales | Ad → long-form sales page → order form (no lead magnet) |
| Info 1 — Lead Magnet + Tripwire + Core + Upsell | Link-post ad → opt-in → tripwire (<$20) → core offer → upsell |
| Info 2 — Webinar / Live Event | Video ad → registration page → thank-you page (high-ticket $500+) |
| Local 1 & 2 — Discount/Incentive to Landing Page | Website-conversion ad (video or image link-post) → coupon/giveaway landing page |

## Find a Solution / craft the hook & offer checklist
- [ ] Review notes on the market's desires and pain points.
- [ ] Force-rank those pain points and desires.
- [ ] Separate **wants** from **needs**.
- [ ] Devise solutions your hook will PROMISE and your offer will FULFILL.
- [ ] Ensure the offer fulfills the hook's promise but stays **INCOMPLETE**.
- [ ] Work backward: end-goal/core offer first, then offer, then hook.

## Pitfalls
- Don't give away the whole solution in the lead magnet (Incompleteness Principle).
- Don't send cold traffic straight to a catalog or bare coupon.
- Don't run a straight CTA-to-buy video to cold traffic.
- Always use a thank-you page (Facebook on-platform coupons no longer allowed; also filters giveaway lead quality).
- Webinar registration is only half the battle — they must register, show up, AND consume.
