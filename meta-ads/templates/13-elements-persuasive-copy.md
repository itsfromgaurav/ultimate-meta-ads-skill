# Template — The 13 Elements of Persuasive Ad Copy

> Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)* — Ralph Burns / Dominate Web Media.
> These are copywriting "seasonings" to spice up copy. **Use sparingly — you do NOT need all of them in every ad.** They reinforce hooks and offers; ad copy plays a **supporting** role to a great video. (Curiosity was the original 14th element, now its own Building Block in Ch. 18.)

Work down the list, applying each element only where it strengthens the ad. Tick the box and fill the note.

---

- [ ] **1. Be Authentic** — write in your OWN voice; don't copy verbatim. Authentic AND effective beats merely effective. Call out the avatar + a pain point (Policy-compliant).
  `Your authentic voice note: ______`

- [ ] **2. Simplify** — one word instead of two/three; prefer one-syllable words; low reading level. (50% of US adults can't read at 8th-grade level.)
  `Words to cut/simplify: ______`

- [ ] **3. Write for Homer (Simpson), not Hawking** — clear, concise, cut-to-the-chase at ~5th-grade reading level to "stop the thumb scroll."
  `Reading-level check: ______`

- [ ] **4. Show Proof** — for COLD audiences, insert credibility statements throughout, each tied to a pain point (usually longer copy). Make the incredible credible.
  `Proof points: ______`

- [ ] **5. Ask Questions** — open the FIRST line of post copy with a question addressing your hook, then RESTATE that question as a benefit in the headline. (The first line of post copy is the most important line in the ad.)
  ```
  First line (question):  [Looking for an easy way to get more superfoods?]
  Headline (benefit):     [Get all your healthy superfoods in one drink.]
  ```

- [ ] **6. Answer Immediately** — give the answer on the next line, or tell them exactly what to do next. People want to be led. "Show people you can help them by actually helping them."
  `Immediate answer/next step: ______`

- [ ] **7. Use Revealing Words** — words implying discovery/novelty (Discover, hidden, astonishing) but NOT hyperbole. Action verbs over adjectives ≥ 2:1. Avoid superlatives (best, finest, outstanding).
  `Revealing words used: ______`

- [ ] **8. "You" Rules** — second person creates ownership; default to it. NEVER tie "you" to a personal attribute (Policy violation). Don't overuse (creepy).
  - ❌ "Do you want to lose weight?" / "Do you have diabetes?"
  - ✅ "superfoods are some of the highest vibing food you will ever come across."

- [ ] **9. Non-Standard Callouts** — frame novel/important points with brackets or parentheses.
  `[Special Discount]   [Don't miss your opportunity]`

- [ ] **10. Capitalize** — capitalize ONLY the one word/phrase to remember. Never all-caps everything (scammy; Policy notices).
  `FREE TRAINING`

- [ ] **11. Embrace the Ellipsis** — create a broken thought needing resolution on the next line; underscores scarcity/importance.
  `click here to get your FREE copy . . . while they last`

- [ ] **12. Mix It Up** — split-test copy lengths (see tiers below).

- [ ] **13. Employ Emojis** — punctuate benefits and the main message; don't overdo it. Split tests show ↑CTR, ↑Relevance Score, ↓CPC, ↓CPA.
  `Emoji placement: ______`

## Copy-length tiers (Element 12 — split-test 3–4 versions)
| Tier | Length | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Ultra-short | 1–2 lines | Strong video does the selling |
| Short | 3–5 lines | Pairs with strong video ads |
| Long | 5+ lines | Image ads selling a core product / consult call; cold-traffic big asks |
| Long-ass | up to 1,000+ words | Heavy persuasion for big cold-traffic asks |

Rule of thumb: a solid selling video needs LESS copy; image ads selling a core product/consult call need LONGER copy; long copy fits big asks for cold traffic.

## Compliance reminders
- Don't use direct claims/promises about the audience (e.g., "Lose 30 lbs in 30 Days" gets banned). Share YOUR OWN results instead ("how I lost 28 lbs in six weeks").
- Don't tie "you" to a personal attribute. Don't overdo all-caps. Don't exaggerate/use hyperbole. Don't copy others' copy verbatim.
