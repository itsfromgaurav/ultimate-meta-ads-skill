# Template — The 7 Power Questions (Ryan Deiss)

> Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*, Ch. 20.
> Self-check questions to run BEFORE finishing any copy/ad. Single underlying goal: **FOCUSED ATTENTION** — if people don't focus, it won't convert. You do NOT need all seven; answering even one or two WELL produces far more powerful copy and beats most competitors. Caveat: this amplifies attention, so the underlying product/service must actually be good.

Answer each question for your current ad.

---

### Q1 — How do I make the offer appear NOVEL, UNIQUE, DISTINCTIVE (new)?
Humans are biologically wired to give focused attention to the unfamiliar (survival instinct). If the offer isn't actually new, ask: (1) How can it *feel* new and noteworthy? (2) How can it be *made* new? (3) What's a different angle? (4) Are there current news stories to attach it to?
Proof: "Major announcement" is one of the best email open-rate subject lines; cable news exploits this with "Breaking News" banners.

### Q2 — How do I make the offer SIMPLE and EASY to understand?
When people grasp something quickly, they like it more AND ascribe more validity to it ("if I understand it, it must be true"). Use rhythm/rhyme. Simplifying means pulling back on claims and not explaining every nuance.

### Q3 — What OPENING QUESTION, when answered, triggers a desire for consistency and drives action?
(Cialdini, *Pre-Suasion*.) General questions engage the mind, not identity. BETTER questions make prospects affirm something about *themselves*, then point that self-image at the desired action. Choose questions whose honest "yes" *qualifies* the prospect.
```
Opener: "Pardon me — do you consider yourself to be a helpful person?"
        "Great! If so, I can really use your help..."
```

### Q4 — How do I PRE-EXPOSE the audience to a concept linked to a desired emotional stimulus?
Before the ask, plant a concept (in image or words) that triggers the emotional state required for the purchase decision — even if logically unrelated. (Why perfume ads "sell sex.")
1. Identify the desired emotional stimulus: how do you want them to feel in order to buy?
2. Pre-expose them to a concept that evokes that emotion *before* the offer.
Example: DigitalMarketer used an iPhone low-battery image (~10% red sliver) to transfer urgency to an expiring sale.

### Q5 — What MENTAL LINKS and ASSOCIATIONS (memory, nostalgia, identity) can I tap and tie to the offer?
Where Q4 targets emotion, Q5 targets memory and identity. Ask: what nostalgia, metaphor, shared experience, or memory can be positively associated with the offer? Use metaphors and imagery (even subtle background images).
Example: Coca-Cola associates "brown sugar water" with family, friends, and good times.

### Q6 — How can OPEN LOOPS hold attention and leverage the close for cognitive closure?
Zeigarnik effect — weave the sales message MID-story and let story closure land AT the point of sale. Closing the story too early switches off attention (the "waiter who forgot the order").

### Q7 — How do I create a visual/mental PORTAL the prospect passes through that opens them to new opportunities?
A "portal" moves the prospect (visually, mentally, or via audio) from one state to another, resetting attention so they're more open to a decision. Without a portal, the audience is less likely to make a change.
- Visual: marketer talking to you while driving in his car (you "ride along").
- Walk-through: "Hi, I'm Fred… come inside, I want you to meet some people" (camera follows through the door).
- Auditory: change/start background music as the transition occurs.
- Iconic: Dorothy's doorway shifting black-and-white to color in *Wizard of Oz*.

## Pre-publish copy checklist
- [ ] Does the offer appear novel, unique, and distinctive?
- [ ] Is the offer simple and easy to understand?
- [ ] Is there an opening question that triggers consistency/action?
- [ ] Have I pre-exposed the audience to a desired emotional concept?
- [ ] Have I tapped mental links/associations (memory, nostalgia, identity)?
- [ ] Have I opened a loop to hold attention until the close?
- [ ] Have I created a mental/visual portal to new opportunity?
