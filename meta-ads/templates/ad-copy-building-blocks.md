# Template — The 7 Ad Building Blocks (Facebook Ad Builder)

> Source: *Ultimate Guide to Facebook Advertising (3rd ed.)*, Ch. 18.
> A modular, Lego-style set of copy components. Mix, match, and reorder to build winning ads, generate test variations, and reduce ad fatigue. **Not every ad needs all seven**, and they can run in any order (e.g., Block 3 can open the copy while Block 1 becomes the link description).

## How to use this template
1. Brainstorm AS MANY options as possible for each block on a notepad/whiteboard FIRST — build a deep inventory of components.
2. Assemble + test variations quickly by swapping blocks.
3. Copy plays a **supporting** role to a great video/creative.

---

## Block 1 — Curiosity
> Curiosity-based hooks grab attention and keep people reading/watching. Techniques: ask a thought-provoking question, use a shocking statement, or contradict conventional wisdom.

```
[Thought-provoking question OR shocking statement OR contrarian claim]
```
Example pattern: `Is breakfast REALLY the most important meal of the day?`

---

## Block 2 — Challenge / Frustration / Pain or Desire
> Call out the audience's biggest challenge or desire in the OPENING line. Source it **verbatim from surveys** (responses are often nearly identical with slight variation).

```
[Avatar's #1 challenge/frustration OR deepest desire, pulled word-for-word from a survey]
```
Survey-line example pulled verbatim: `scaling your business with max ROI and minimum wasted money`

**Two deep-dive survey questions to harvest this block:**
```
(1) Frustration/Challenge: What's your number-one single biggest challenge or
frustration with [doing X] right now? (Please be as detailed and specific as
possible. Go beyond saying "too confusing" or "too expensive.")

(2) Desire: Why do you want to [achieve Y]? What is the biggest reason why you
want to master this or have someone master it for you?
```

---

## Block 3 — Benefits and Desires
> Surface the DEEP desire by appending **"so you can"** / **"so that"** to any benefit. Speak to the conversation in the prospect's mind, not the product features.
> ⚠️ Benefit claims can trip Facebook Ad Policy — read the policy.

```
[Feature/benefit] so you can [deeper desire / end benefit].
```
Example: Samcart sells checkout software, but the ad sells "doubling your sales."

---

## Block 4 — Credibility or Authority Statement
> Why should people listen to, trust, and obey you? Inherited/borrowed credibility counts. Avoid sounding boastful.

```
[Proof of authority — results, experience, borrowed credibility, e.g., "After managing $X in ad spend..."]
```

---

## Block 5 — Give an "Aha Moment"
> Reveal an insight or expose a common belief as a myth / inaccurate / recently-changed. Builds instant trust, makes the ad shareable, and is HUGE for the algorithm (cheaper clicks/impressions, more reach, higher ROI).

```
[The myth / common belief] → [the surprising truth or recent change]
```

---

## Block 6 — Call-to-Action (CTA)
> Make the next action crystal clear; people are skimming. For video ads ~3 min+, get at least a SOFT CTA in the first 60–90 seconds.

```
[Exactly what to do next — e.g., "Click below to download your free [asset]."]
```

---

## Block 7 — Overcoming Objections (mini-FAQ)
> Place a mini-FAQ AFTER the first CTA. Increases conversions, trust, and relevance score even when it lengthens the ad. "It flat out works."

```
Q: [Common objection 1]?
A: [Reassuring answer]

Q: [Common objection 2]?
A: [Reassuring answer]
```
